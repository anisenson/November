Thomas Edison

Early Years


Birth and Childhood: Thomas Edison was born on February 11, 1847, in Milan, Ohio, to Samuel and Nancy Edison.

Early Education: He had limited formal education due to hearing problems but showed an early interest in experiments and scientific inquiry.

Work as a Telegraph Operator: Edison started working as a telegraph operator, where he gained experience and knowledge in electrical engineering.

Major Accomplishments


Invention of the Phonograph: In 1877, Edison invented the phonograph, the first device to both record and reproduce sound.

Electric Light Bulb: His most famous invention, the practical incandescent electric light bulb, patented in 1879, revolutionized indoor lighting and made him a household name.

Motion Picture Camera: Edison's work also contributed to the development of the motion picture camera, leading to advancements in the film industry.
